Is Dressmaker a Multiplayer Game?

  • No, Dressmaker is exclusively a single-player game.
  • Currently, there are no multiplayer, co-op, or split-screen modes available in either the itch.io prototype or the planned Steam full version.
Learn How to Play Solo

Understanding the Core Game Modes

Dressmaker is built from the ground up to be a relaxing, independent cozy simulation game. The core gameplay loop focuses on your personal creative journey: reviewing customer notebook requests, carefully cutting fabrics, and mastering the sewing machine at your own pace.

Because the game relies heavily on precise mouse/keyboard movements (like following the grain line for a 100% sewing quality score), the developers at Free Lives and Elyaradine have focused their efforts entirely on a polished single-player experience rather than network synchronization.

Can You Play Dressmaker Offline?

Yes! Since the game lacks multiplayer servers or online leaderboards, Dressmaker is completely playable offline. Once you download the game to your Windows, Mac, or Linux computer, you do not need an active internet connection to measure fabrics, tailor dresses, or satisfy your in-game customers.

💡 Quick Tip

While the game is offline, you might initially need an internet connection to bypass the Mac "Malicious Software" warning during your very first launch. If you run into this, check our troubleshooting guide.

Will Co-op Be Added in the Future?

As of the latest Developer updates and the upcoming Steam release features, there are no official announcements regarding a future co-op mode. The roadmap currently prioritizes expanding single-player content: more clothing patterns, higher variety of fabrics, and deeper customer interactions.
