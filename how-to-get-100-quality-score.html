How to Get a 100% Sewing Quality Score in Dressmaker

Achieving a perfect 100% sewing score requires mastering the game's realistic momentum and dual-axis mouse controls. To maximize your score, you must delicately balance your speed while keeping the fabric perfectly aligned with the dotted grain line.

🕹️ Vertical Mouse = Speed

Move your mouse UP to accelerate the needle, and DOWN to slow the machine down to a crawl.

↔️ Horizontal Mouse = Direction

Move your mouse LEFT and RIGHT to pivot the fabric and steer the dotted line under the needle.

1. Slow Down on Curves and Corners

The most common reason players lose Quality Score is taking corners too fast. Dressmaker's sewing machine has built-in momentum. If you try to aggressively turn the fabric while the needle is moving at top speed, you will stray off the dotted line.

The Trick: As you approach a curve or a sharp corner, physically pull your mouse down towards yourself to drastically lower the needle speed. Once the machine is ticking slowly (one stitch at a time), gently adjust the horizontal pivot.

2. Beware of Mouse Sensitivity

The game's steering mechanism is highly sensitive. Your quality score can drop from 100% to 95% in a fraction of a second if you jerk the mouse too hard.

  • Micro-adjustments: Make tiny, smooth lateral movements rather than sweeping arcs.
  • Align before accelerating: Ensure the dotted line is perfectly straight beneath the needle before pushing your mouse up to gain speed on long, straight seams.

3. How to "Undo" a Bad Seam

A frequently asked question in the community is: "Is there a seam-rip or undo button?"

Currently, there is no seam-rip function. If your score drops to 80% and you want another shot at perfection, the only way to retry is to intentionally fail the current seam. Steer the needle completely off the fabric to "ruin" the stitch; the game will discard the ruined thread and allow you to re-do that specific seam from the beginning. While slightly tedious, it's the only way to guarantee a 100% final garment rating.

4. Practice Makes Perfect

Remember that Dressmaker is designed with an intentional skill curve. Your first few dresses might score 70-80%, and that's completely fine! Completing orders, even imperfect ones, grants you money to buy more patterns from the shop. As your muscle memory improves, 100% quality scores will become second nature.
